Blank Matrices
Blank Whole Blood, Serum, Plasma, Urine — Real & Synthetic
▼
"We're validating a new method and need blank matrix to spike."
That's exactly what these are for. Blank Whole Blood (44600-WB), Blank Human Serum (44600), Normal Human Serum (44600-S(500)), Defibrinated Human Plasma (44600-D) — all from FDA-licensed US facilities, screened for common drugs of abuse and alcohol. You're starting from a verified clean baseline, not hoping your pooled source is what it says it is.
"What anticoagulant is in the whole blood?"
Potassium oxalate with sodium fluoride for preservation. The standard for forensic whole blood work — especially important for volatiles, where your anticoagulant and preservative choices actually affect analyte stability.
"We need blank urine."
Certified Drug Free Urine in frozen (88121-CDF(F)) or liquid (88121-CDF(L)). Screened for the major DOA analytes. Frozen has no preservatives. Liquid uses sodium azide. Depends on your storage setup and how you're using it.
"We're running analytes that are really sensitive to matrix interference — endogenous compounds keep causing problems."
That's actually where our SMx line comes in. SMx Oral Fluid, SMx Serum, SMx Urine — synthetic matrices designed to mimic real human matrix while eliminating the unwanted endogenous analytes that cause those interference headaches. When you need consistency and reliability across lots for difficult analytes, SMx gives you that clean, controlled baseline that real human matrix sometimes can't. Works on LC-MS/MS, GC-MS, QTOF, immunoassay, all of it. It's not a substitute for real matrix — it's the right tool for a specific problem.
